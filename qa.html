QA Testing – Nation.dev Platform

View platform ↗

APL project · Manual testing · UX focus · Structured bug reporting

During my internship (APL), I tested a platform under development called Nation.dev. We were asked to explore the system and report bugs, but without any predefined structure or testing method.

Since I enjoy working in a structured and systematic way, I created my own approach for both testing and documenting findings. I focused on understanding the user flow, identifying edge cases, and describing issues clearly from a user perspective.

My approach

  • explored the platform from a user perspective
  • tested different flows and scenarios
  • documented findings in a clear and structured format
  • focused on UX, validation, and feedback

Example findings

Below are a few examples of issues I identified and documented during testing:

Missing feedback when character limit is reached

Description: The input field silently stops accepting text when the maximum length is reached, without notifying the user.

Impact: Users may believe their full input was saved.

Suggested improvement: Add a character counter or clear message when the limit is reached.

No warning when navigating away with unsaved changes

Description: Navigating away from a page without saving results in lost data, without any warning.

Impact: Risk of losing longer text and a negative user experience.

Suggested improvement: Add a confirmation dialog or autosave functionality.

What I learned

This experience made me realize how much I enjoy working with testing and user experience, especially identifying issues from a user perspective and structuring findings in a clear and useful way.

It also showed me how well my interest in UX and usability, together with my structured and detail-oriented approach, fit with QA work.
